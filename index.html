SURJ Colorado Springs Mission Statement

SURJ Colorado Springs honors SURJ National's core principles of calling in more white people to be accomplices in the fight for racial justice and holding white people accountable and responsible for dismantling white supremacy and breaking down systems of oppression. Simultaneously, SURJ COS has been a multiracial space since its inception and we want to further embrace and center our community. ALL are welcome that are wanting to show up for racial justice. At its core, SURJ is accountable to people of color (POC)-led activism, initiatives, and calls-to-action and we will maintain this format.

May Community Meeting: Islam Q&A

Date: Sunday May 7
Time: 2:30pm - 4:30pm
Place: Penrose Library, Carnegie Room
20 N Cascade Ave, Colorado Springs, CO 80903

The Islamic Society of Colorado Springs and Showing Up for Racial Justice Colorado Springs present Arshad Yousufi and Kamel Elwazier along with other guests from our local Mosque to share personal stories and present some history and basic facts about Islam and Muslims to address common questions, misperceptions, and “alternative facts”. A question and answer session will follow. Please join us for this opportunity to connect, learn how we can all be better neighbors, friends, and supporters of our fellow American Muslim brothers and sisters, and come away with accurate information and a better understanding to help combat hate, fear of the unknown, and the climate of intolerance in the US.

No admission, but donations will be taken for local women's shelters and organizations that work with the homeless.
